Abu Dhabi Travel Forecast Insights

Department of Culture and Tourism - Abu Dhabi

Strategic Analysis & Recommendations Based on 2025 Travel Trends

Executive Summary

This report presents key insights from Google's 2025 travel forecast reports, analyzing global travel trends, booking behaviors, and destination preferences with specific implications for Abu Dhabi tourism.

The findings reveal that travel demand maintains positive momentum in 2025 (+1% YoY growth), with travelers increasingly open to new destinations (46%) and willing to spend more on accommodation (+22%), activities (+19%), and transportation (+18%) compared to 2024.

Abu Dhabi is uniquely positioned to capitalize on these trends due to its blend of cultural experiences and world-class entertainment, strategic location as a global hub, premium-value proposition, and family-friendly infrastructure. This document translates these insights into actionable strategies to enhance Abu Dhabi's position in the global tourism market.

EMEA Report Global Travel Intent US Report

Research Methodology

This analysis is based on three comprehensive Google travel reports:

EMEA Report "EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends" - Provides detailed insights into travel behavior across European markets, focusing on booking patterns, destination preferences, and spending intentions for Spring and Summer 2025.

Global Travel Intent "Global Travel Intent Monthly Travel Surveys March 2025" - Offers a worldwide perspective on travel intentions across different regions including Europe, North America, Middle East, Asia Pacific, and Latin America, with detailed breakdowns of planning status, booking channels, and travel motivations.

US Report "US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends" - Focuses specifically on the American market, analyzing consumer spending power, travel prioritization, and emerging travel habits among US travelers.

The data was collected through Google's internal analysis of travel clicks, hotel ad interactions, and YouTube travel content views, supplemented by consumer surveys across multiple markets. The research captures both quantitative metrics on travel intent and qualitative insights on traveler preferences and decision-making factors.

Global Travel Market Performance

The research identified several key trends in the global travel market for 2025:

  • Travel Demand Growth: HIGH - Travel demand maintains positive momentum with +1% YoY growth in January 2025, continuing the upward trend from 2024 (+5% FY-2024 vs FY-2023). EMEA
  • Hotel Engagement: HIGH - Hotel Ads clicks have seen significant growth across markets (+10-26% YoY), indicating strong intent to book accommodations. EMEA
  • Digital Inspiration: HIGH - YouTube has become increasingly important for travel inspiration with 14% YoY growth in travel content views, reaching over 1 billion views. EMEA US
  • Spending Priority: HIGH - Travel is expected to be the #1 discretionary spending item for consumers worldwide in 2025, outranking electronics, home improvements, and fashion. EMEA US

EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends

The EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends report reveals continued growth in travel demand across European markets, with distinct booking patterns and destination preferences. The report highlights significant opportunities for capturing both early planners and last-minute bookers, with many travelers still finalizing their Spring and Summer plans.

Source: Google EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

Booking Behavior & Timing

Opportunity: HIGH

71% of Spring travelers haven't fully booked their trips yet, presenting a significant opportunity to influence decisions. Book-to-travel windows have increased YoY (81 days for flights, 61 days for accommodations), showing travelers are planning further ahead. However, 18% of travelers book within 2 weeks of their Easter holiday, indicating a dual opportunity for both early and late booking campaigns.

Key Market Nuances: 33% of Summer flight searches occur during the run-up to Easter, creating an overlap between late Spring bookings and early Summer planning. This presents a unique opportunity to capture both immediate and future travel intent simultaneously.

Source: Google EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

Spending Patterns & Priorities

Opportunity: HIGH

Spring travelers are more likely to spend more on accommodation (+22%), activities (+19%), and transportation (+18%) compared to 2024. This increased spending willingness aligns with Abu Dhabi's premium tourism offerings and presents opportunities to highlight luxury experiences and exclusive attractions.

Key Market Nuances: Despite this willingness to spend more, pricing remains the top priority for travelers across all regions (NA: 21%, ME: 23%, EU: 20%), followed by brand reputation and trust (16-19%). This suggests a need to emphasize value rather than just luxury, highlighting the exceptional quality-to-price ratio of Abu Dhabi experiences.

Source: Google EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

Emerging Travel Preferences

Opportunity: MEDIUM

Sustainability remains important to travelers in 2025, with many planning to incorporate eco-friendly practices into their trips. Additionally, 32% of surveyed users used or are interested in using AI to plan vacations, indicating a shift toward technology-assisted travel planning.

Key Market Nuances: The Spring travel period consists of "micro-peaks" across markets, including Easter holidays, Spring Break, and various regional holidays. Many of these peaks are later in 2025 compared to 2024, requiring adjusted marketing timelines to capture demand effectively.

Source: Google EMEA Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

Global Travel Intent Monthly Surveys (March 2025)

The Global Travel Intent Monthly Surveys provide a comprehensive view of travel intentions across different regions, with detailed insights into planning status, booking channels, and travel motivations. The March 2025 survey reveals important regional differences in travel behavior that can inform Abu Dhabi's marketing strategies.

Source: Google Global Travel Intent Monthly Travel Surveys March 2025

Regional Travel Intent

Opportunity: HIGH

34% of Middle East travelers plan to travel in the next 6 months, with 28% traveling with family and 23% taking solo trips. Additionally, 26% have not started planning their international trips, presenting an opportunity for early influence. Middle East travelers prefer booking through OTA websites (18%), direct suppliers (16%), and traditional agencies (16%).

Key Market Nuances: 35% of Middle East travelers plan to take shorter trips and reduce budgets due to cost concerns. This suggests an opportunity for Abu Dhabi to position itself as a premium but accessible destination for regional travelers, emphasizing value and proximity for shorter getaways.

Source: Google Global Travel Intent Monthly Travel Surveys March 2025

Trip Types & Companions

Opportunity: HIGH

Travelers in North America and Europe are mainly planning trips with family (NA: 30%, EU: 27%) or traveling with a partner (NA: 23%, EU: 25%). Middle East travelers will mainly be traveling with family (28%) or taking solo trips (23%). This family-focused travel trend aligns well with Abu Dhabi's family-friendly attractions and accommodations.

Key Market Nuances: When going on leisure trips, most travelers focus on relaxing and unwinding & visiting friends and family, EU (24%), ME (17%) and NA (16%). This suggests an opportunity to highlight Abu Dhabi's blend of relaxation and cultural experiences.

Source: Google Global Travel Intent Monthly Travel Surveys March 2025

Booking Channels & Inflation Impact

Opportunity: MEDIUM

Cost of living remains a top concern for 41% of travelers, with varying impacts across regions. With rising travel costs, 40% of NA travelers plan to reduce their budgets and take shorter trips. 35% of ME travelers also plan to take shorter trips & reduce budget. However, 33% of EU travelers plan to maintain the same budget as last trip.

Key Market Nuances: Pricing remains priority for travelers across all regions (NA: 21%, ME: 23%, EU: 20%). A brand's reputation & trust is also a key deciding factor for 16% of travelers in NA, 19% in EU and 16% in ME. This suggests a need for Abu Dhabi to emphasize both value and trustworthiness in its marketing.

Source: Google Global Travel Intent Monthly Travel Surveys March 2025

US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends

The US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends report focuses specifically on the American market, analyzing consumer spending power, travel prioritization, and emerging travel habits among US travelers. The report reveals strong growth in travel demand and spending, with particular opportunities among specific demographic segments.

Source: Google US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

US Travel Demand & Consumption

Opportunity: HIGH

US travel demand showed strong growth in 2024, with 5.5% more travel clicks and 17% more watch time for travel content on YouTube compared to the previous year. Consumer spending on travel has increased by 20% since 2019 (adjusted for inflation), indicating that travel remains a top priority despite economic concerns.

Key Market Nuances: Travel is a top priority for US consumers in the next 3 months (32%), outranking most other discretionary spending categories except dining out. Additionally, 33% of those expecting a tax refund plan to use it on travel, with 60% of refunds expected to be filed in March or April, creating a significant opportunity for spring and summer bookings.

Source: Google US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

US Seasonal Travel Patterns

Opportunity: HIGH

US travelers show distinct seasonal patterns with two peak seasons: Spring (domestic peak: week of 3/25, international peak: week of 3/18) and Summer (domestic peaks: weeks of 5/13, 6/24, 7/22, 8/19). The week of 6/24 was identified as the most-coveted week for travel in 2024, presenting a strategic opportunity for targeted marketing.

Key Market Nuances: 83% of summer domestic travel demand and 62% of international demand was still up for grabs after March, indicating that US travelers make summer travel decisions relatively late. This presents a significant opportunity for influencing summer travel plans during the spring season.

Source: Google US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

US Consumer Behavior & Demographics

Opportunity: HIGH

32% of US travelers expect to travel more in 2025 than in 2024, with particularly strong intent among Millennials (42%), Gen Z (36%), parents (39%), urban residents (44%), and high-income households (40%). The majority of US consumers plan to travel in the summer of 2025, especially Gen Z travelers who show stronger interest in travel overall.

Key Market Nuances: Gen Z travelers represent a particularly valuable segment, with 52% reporting splurging on travel expenses (+23pp vs. overall) and 36% planning to travel more in 2025. They are also more inclined to travel internationally, taking nearly as many international trips as domestic (2.1 vs. 2.3), a much tighter spread than the overall population (1.9 vs. 2.4).

Source: Google US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

US International Travel Hotspots

Opportunity: MEDIUM

The strong USD provides fuel for international travel, with US travelers showing increased interest in destinations where their currency has more purchasing power. Emerging hotspots for US travelers in 2025 include Japan, Vietnam, and Taiwan, with significant growth in search clicks, YouTube views, and Hotel Ads clicks for these destinations.

Key Market Nuances: Content driving US travelers' interest in these destinations includes culinary experiences, unique attractions, video blogs, and cultural education content. For Japan, content about cultural experiences and food is particularly popular. For Vietnam, content about attainable luxury and unique experiences resonates well. For Taiwan, culinary experiences and "underrated destination" recommendations drive interest.

Source: Google US Spring & Summer 2025 Forecast & Trends Report

Strategic Recommendations for Abu Dhabi

1. Implement Dual-Phase Marketing Strategy

High Priority

With 71% of Spring travelers yet to fully book their trips and 18% booking within two weeks of travel, Abu Dhabi has an opportunity to capture both early planners and last-minute bookers simultaneously.

EMEA US
Key Actions:
  • Launch immediate "Book Now" campaigns targeting late Spring bookers with limited-time offers and simplified booking processes
  • Simultaneously run "Plan Your Summer" inspiration campaigns targeting the 33% of travelers searching for Summer trips during Spring
  • Create urgency-driven content for last-minute bookers while providing detailed planning resources for advance bookers
  • Develop flexible packages that accommodate both short booking windows (2 weeks) and extended planning horizons (81 days)
  • Implement dynamic pricing strategies that reward early booking while maintaining competitive last-minute offers

2. Enhance Digital Discovery & Inspiration

High Priority

With YouTube seeing 14% YoY growth in travel content views and 46% of travelers open to new destinations, Abu Dhabi must strengthen its digital presence to capture the attention of potential visitors during their inspiration phase.

EMEA US
Key Actions:
  • Invest in high-quality video content showcasing Abu Dhabi's diverse offerings across cultural sites, entertainment destinations, and natural attractions
  • Develop market-specific content strategies with stronger presence on YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok
  • Create immersive virtual tours and 360° experiences of key attractions to engage potential visitors
  • Partner with travel influencers and content creators to reach new audiences and build authentic destination narratives
  • Implement an AI-powered travel planner on the Visit Abu Dhabi website that creates personalized itineraries based on traveler preferences

3. Target US Market with Strategic Positioning

Medium Priority

American travelers are showing growing interest in international destinations, particularly Gen Z travelers who are more inclined to travel internationally and splurge on travel experiences. This presents an opportunity for Abu Dhabi to position itself as an exciting destination for this demographic.

US
Key Actions:
  • Partner with Etihad Airways to promote Abu Dhabi as a premium stopover or extension for US travelers heading to or from Asia
  • Develop "Middle East Experience" marketing that positions Abu Dhabi as the ideal entry point to the region
  • Create targeted campaigns for Gen Z travelers highlighting Abu Dhabi's unique blend of tradition and modernity
  • Highlight Abu Dhabi's English-speaking environment, Western amenities, and safety to address potential concerns of US travelers
  • Leverage the growing trend of "bleisure" travel (business + leisure) to attract US business travelers to extend their stays

4. Develop Family-Focused Regional Strategy

Medium Priority

With 28% of Middle East travelers planning family trips and 35% looking to take shorter, more budget-conscious journeys, Abu Dhabi can position itself as the ideal regional family destination.

Global
Key Actions:
  • Create comprehensive family packages that bundle accommodations with multiple attractions at value pricing
  • Develop targeted campaigns highlighting Abu Dhabi's world-class family attractions (Ferrari World, Warner Bros. World, Yas Waterworld)
  • Implement "Kids Go Free" or similar promotions during key regional school holiday periods
  • Enhance digital content showcasing family-friendly cultural experiences that combine entertainment with educational value
  • Partner with regional airlines to offer family-specific deals for short getaways

5. Highlight Sustainability Initiatives

Low Priority

Sustainability remains important to travelers in 2025, with many planning to incorporate eco-friendly practices into their trips. Abu Dhabi can leverage its existing sustainability initiatives to appeal to this growing segment.

EMEA
Key Actions:
  • Showcase Abu Dhabi's sustainability initiatives, including Masdar City, mangrove conservation efforts, and sustainable tourism practices
  • Develop eco-friendly tour options that highlight the emirate's natural attractions and conservation efforts
  • Create content highlighting sustainable transportation options within Abu Dhabi
  • Partner with eco-conscious travel influencers to promote Abu Dhabi's sustainability credentials
  • Implement a certification program for tourism businesses in Abu Dhabi that meet specific sustainability criteria

Abu Dhabi's Competitive Advantages

Key Competitive Strengths

  1. Cultural-Entertainment Balance: Few destinations offer Abu Dhabi's combination of deep cultural experiences (Louvre Abu Dhabi, traditional heritage sites) alongside world-class entertainment (Yas Island attractions).
  2. Geographic Connectivity: Abu Dhabi's position as a global hub between East and West makes it ideal for capturing the growing stopover and multi-destination travel trend.
  3. Premium-Value Proposition: Abu Dhabi offers luxury experiences often at better value than comparable destinations in Europe or North America.
  4. Family-Friendly Infrastructure: The emirate's comprehensive family offerings align perfectly with the dominant travel companion trend in key source markets.

Strategic Market Opportunities

  • Capture Late Booking Demand: Target the 18% of travelers who book within 2 weeks of travel with streamlined booking processes and last-minute packages.
  • Leverage YouTube for Destination Marketing: Capitalize on the 14% YoY growth in travel content views with immersive video content.
  • Target Family Travelers from Middle East Markets: Focus on the 28% of ME travelers planning family trips with tailored offerings.
  • Implement AI-Powered Travel Planning: Address the 32% of travelers interested in AI travel planning with innovative digital tools.

Core Strategic Insight

"Abu Dhabi is uniquely positioned at the intersection of increased travel spending, openness to new destinations, and desire for premium experiences that deliver genuine value."

This insight encapsulates the central opportunity identified in the research: Abu Dhabi can capitalize on travelers' increased willingness to spend on quality experiences (accommodation +22%, activities +19%) while simultaneously addressing their price sensitivity by emphasizing the exceptional value proposition of the destination.

Key Performance Metrics to Track

  • Conversion Rate: Percentage increase in bookings from targeted campaigns
  • Market Share Growth: Particularly from US and regional Middle East markets
  • Digital Engagement: Growth in engagement with Abu Dhabi content across platforms
  • Average Spend Per Visitor: Tracking the success of value-based positioning
  • Booking Lead Time: Monitoring changes in how far in advance visitors book

Conclusion

The 2025 travel forecast reveals a golden opportunity for Abu Dhabi. With travel demand growing steadily (+1% YoY) and 71% of Spring travelers still finalizing their plans, the timing couldn't be better to capture market share.

What makes this moment particularly promising is the fascinating paradox in consumer behavior: travelers are simultaneously willing to spend more on quality experiences (accommodation +22%, activities +19%) while remaining acutely price-conscious. This creates the perfect environment for Abu Dhabi's unique value proposition - premium experiences that deliver genuine value rather than just luxury.

The US market presents especially compelling opportunities, with 32% of American travelers planning to increase their travel in 2025 and Gen Z travelers showing remarkable willingness to splurge on international experiences. By targeting these high-potential segments with the right messaging at the right time, Abu Dhabi can significantly boost tourism arrivals and spending.

Success will require nimble execution across multiple fronts: capturing late bookers with streamlined offers, developing compelling video content for the growing YouTube audience, positioning Abu Dhabi as an ideal stopover for US travelers, and highlighting family-friendly offerings for regional visitors. With these targeted approaches, Abu Dhabi can transform these market insights into tangible tourism growth throughout 2025.
